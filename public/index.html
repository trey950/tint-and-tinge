The Method

Three dimensions. Twelve seasons. One palette that's yours.

Hue

Warm undertones glow against gold; cool undertones come alive against silver. We map your temperature first.

Value

Are you naturally light and airy, or rich and saturated? We measure how deep your features want to go.

Chroma

Bright and clear, or soft and muted? The clarity of your palette is what makes a color sing or fall flat.

How we calculate your season. Each answer you give scores against the three axes — temperature (warm vs. cool), value (light vs. deep), and chroma (clear vs. soft). Your final coordinate is matched to the closest of twelve color seasons using Euclidean distance, and a confidence rating is derived from how close you land to that season's center. The same diagnostic that an in-person analyst uses, condensed into ten questions you answer honestly in natural light.
